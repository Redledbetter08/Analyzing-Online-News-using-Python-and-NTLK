Overview



In order to make the most of this application, try finding two articles that report on the same story from different news websites or sources. I've included a list of some popular news websites of different political persuasions at the bottom of the page.

All you need to do is copy and paste the URLs to the articles into the boxes in Compare and click compare.

You'll then receive two donut charts that will show you the breakdown of positive, negative and neutral sentiment for both articles. Please note that you should expect to receive a high percentage of neutral sentiment (around 70-90%). The interesting part here is to focus on the difference between positive and negative sentiment.

In addition to the sentiment analysis, I've also calculated some basic syntactic differences between the two articles. These include the number of characters, the number of sentences and the number of quotations used by the articles. Below this is a list of the ten most common words used in each article.

  • The New York Times
  • Fox News
  • Reuters
  • BBC
  • USA Today
  • The Guardian
  • NPR
  • The Hill
  • Breitbart

    • Please note that there are currently problems with reading data from cnn.com and huffingtonpost.com. If you happen to encounter any problems with other websites then please let me know via the contact page!
